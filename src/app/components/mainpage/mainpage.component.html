ABOUT THE PROJECT

Herodotus, hailed as the "Father of History," embarked on remarkable journeys in the 5th century BCE, chronicling the ancient world's wonders. His extensive voyages led him to unravel the mysteries of the Seven Wonders. Roaming the Mediterranean and beyond, Herodotus meticulously documented architectural marvels like the Great Pyramid of Giza, the Hanging Gardens of Babylon, and the Temple of Artemis at Ephesus. His narratives not only unveiled the grandeur of these wonders but also delved into the cultures and stories surrounding them.

Herodotus' pioneering accounts laid the foundation for historical inquiry, preserving the essence of ancient wonders for generations to come. With that, you are know prepared to visit these same wonders from millenia ago, right in here, at you computer.
